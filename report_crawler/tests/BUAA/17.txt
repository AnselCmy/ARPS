Abstract：
The human brain is structurally and functionally organized into a complex network. The topological description of the network has been proposed as “human connectomics”. In the past decade, researchers have demonstrated that using big neuroimaging data [e.g. structural MRI, diffusion MRI and functional MRI (fMRI)], together with sophisticated analytic strategies such as graph theory, it is possible to noninvasively map the patterns of structural and functional connectivity of the human brain. In this talk, Dr. Yong He will present their research works in big neuroimaging data and relevant analysis methods of large-scale brain networks as well as typical applications in healthy and diseased populations. Additionally, he will also talk about some challenging issues in this field.
